UI & Usage

Design Issues

The problem with this microwave is that it is too simple and too complex at the same time. Because it is just a panel of buttons, every piece of functionality must exist on the panel. This means the end-user is inundated with tons of buttons and functionality that may not be relevant to their current action.

Common Uses

Time Cook

The numpad makes time cooking easy.

Sensor Cooking

The vague nature of the presets makes them confusing to use.

Rare Uses

Power level

Changing the power level is not immediately obvious. Unlike other buttons, power level is a mode switch.

Kitchen Timer

Kitchen Timer runs into the same problem as power level.

Common Sequence of Actions

User presses the numbers they want for the cook time, then press Enter/Start

How does the microwave support the user?

The microwave uses the screen to display hints or help.

How does the microwave provide feedback

When pressing a button, a sound is played and the screen also displays feedback for some buttons.

Common Mistakes

I often find that using anything other than time cook is unclear. Because everything is shown at once, I am often not sure the correct way to do things like change the power level.

My Design

Justification

My design splits the functionality of the microwave into two separate screens. This allows me to simplify the design of both screens without losing any functionality. Also, functionality is only shown if it is relevant.
